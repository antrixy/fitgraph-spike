The first Premium+ subscription charge on your credit card will take place at the end of your trial unless you cancel before then. You may cancel in the Settings menu, in the App Store or in Google Play, depending on where you signed up.

Once the trial ends, your subscription will auto-renew unless you cancel. Cancel before the next renewal date to avoid being charged for the upcoming billing cycle. If you purchased a monthly subscription, you will be charged every month. If you purchased an annual subscription, you will be charged every year.

You may cancel your recurring subscription at any time. When you cancel, you are canceling the next billing charge—Premium+ features will remain available to you until the end of your current paid subscription period.

Yes! Meal Planner was specifically designed to fit seamlessly into the MyFitnessPal experience. You can easily log your planned meals to your MyFitnessPal food diary to keep track of your daily macros and calories.

We offer meal plans based on 10 different diet preferences including: low-carb, vegetarian, paleo, balanced, pescatarian, flexitarian, keto, Mediterranean, whole-food focus, and vegan.

Meal Planner is currently available in the following countries: United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, and Australia. Integrations with grocery apps are currently available in the U.S. only.

You can find Meal Planner by downloading the MyFitnessPal app and heading to the "Plan" tab at the bottom of your home screen.

MyFitnessPal Premium+ users in certain countries may be able to sync their Meal Planner grocery list to one or more of the following shopping apps:

  • iOS: Instacart, Walmart, Kroger, Amazon Fresh, Whole Foods
  • Android: Instacart, Walmart

We will update this list as more grocery integrations become available.
